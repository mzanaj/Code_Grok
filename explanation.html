What You Should Know About These Scores

1. Bidirectional Symmetry

üîÑ Important: Conversations Are Treated as Single Units

Alice‚ÜíBob and Bob‚ÜíAlice are combined into one conversation.

  • ‚úÖ Good: Natural way to think about conversations
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Watch out: Can't distinguish who initiated F1 discussions
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Double counting: Both Alice and Bob get credit for the same conversation in their user scores

2. Extreme Behavior Cases

üö® All Old Messages

Scenario: 100 F1 messages from 2 months ago

Result: Score ‚âà 3-5 (heavily decayed)

Why: Time decay reduces influence

üìä Score Clustering

Scenario: Chat with 1 F1 message (score=10)

Result: Chat score = 10

Why: No other messages to average with

üéØ Threshold Effects

Scenario: Many messages scored 2.9

Result: All ignored (below threshold of 3)

Why: Hard cutoff filters noise

üåä Dilution Effect

Scenario: User in 10 chats, only 1 about F1

Result: User score can be low even if that 1 chat is highly relevant

Why: Averaged across all conversations

3. AI Scoring Limitations

ü§ñ What the AI Might Miss

  • Context: "He won!" might be about F1 but lacks explicit keywords
  • Sarcasm/Jokes: "Yeah, F1 is SO exciting üôÑ" might score high despite negative sentiment
  • Language: Non-English F1 discussions might score lower
  • Abbreviations: "Max ver dominated" might score lower than "Max Verstappen dominated"

4. Temporal Edge Cases

üìà When Scores Work Best

  • ‚úÖ Comparing relative interest across users/chats
  • ‚úÖ Tracking trends over time (weekly/monthly)
  • ‚úÖ Identifying your most engaged community members
  • ‚úÖ Spotting when topic interest spikes (e.g., race weekends)

‚ùå When to Be Cautious

  • ‚ùå Absolute score comparisons across different topics
  • ‚ùå Making decisions based on single data points
  • ‚ùå Assuming perfect accuracy in edge cases
  • ‚ùå Treating scores as objective truth vs. useful approximations

5. The "Double Counting" Reality

This is the most important bias to understand:

Scenario: Alice and Bob's F1 Chat

Conversation Score: 9.5 (highly relevant)

Alice's User Score: Gets credit from this 9.5

Bob's User Score: Also gets credit from this 9.5
